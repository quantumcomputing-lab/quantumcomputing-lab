Quantum Quality Assurance Workflow

Experience shows that classical tools sometimes do not carry over seamlessly to the quantum world. Error correction is one example. I think quality assurance is another. Given the intrinsic randomness of quantum mechanics, what do the terms computational accuracy, result reproducibility, device reliability and program stability even mean?

In this short paper (https://www.spiedigitallibrary.org/conference-proceedings-of-spie/12238/1223809/Assessing-the-stability-of-noisy-quantum-computation/10.1117/12.2631809.short?SSO=1), I present a 4-layered workflow for quantum assurance that should help the industry prepare systematically for the coming of dependable (trustworthy? Trust is a completely different 'technical' ballgame in quantum computing - let's avoid that word for now) quantum computers. Will be curious if you have any comments or observations.
